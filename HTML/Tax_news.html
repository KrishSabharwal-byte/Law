India’s New Income Tax Bill, 2025: A Bold Step Toward Simplification

On 11 August 2025, the Lok Sabha passed the Income-Tax (No. 2) Bill, 2025— a comprehensive overhaul oft India’s tax framework, replacing the decades-old Income-Tax Act, 1961

Legislative Path & Background

  • February 13, 2025 : The original Income-Tax Bill was introduced.
  • July 21 : A Parliamentary Select Committee, led by MP Baijayant Panda, submitted its report with 285 recommendations
  • August 8 : The earlier draft was withdrawn to integrate committee feedback.
  • August 11 : The revised bill was tabled and passed amidst vocal opposition, without debate

Pillars of the Reform: “SIMPLE” Framework

The revised bill is built on the guiding principles of SIMPLE :

  • S – Streamlined structure & language
  • I – Integrated and concise
  • M – Minimised litigation
  • P – Practical and transparent
  • L – Learn and adapt
  • E – Efficient tax reform

Key Features & Impact

  • Streamlined Structure : Sections reduced from over 800 to 536, reorganized into 23 chapters and 16 schedules.
  • Tax Year Concept : Replaces the confusing “Previous Year” and “Assessment Year” with a singular, clearer “Tax Year”.
  • Faceless, Digital-First Assessments : Promotes efficiency and transparency, reducing human interface and the scope for corruption.
  • TDS Refunds and Penalties : Enables TDS refunds even after late filings, with no penalties. Also introduces “nil-TDS certificates” for taxpayers with no liability.
  • Clarity and Definitions : Simplifies provisions for house property income, capital assets, MSMEs, “beneficial owner” definitions, pre-construction interest, and pension-related deductions—including commuted pension.
  • Retirement Policy Alignment : Aligns the tax treatment of Unified Pension Scheme (UPS) with National Pension System (NPS), including exemptions and taxes on lump sum and premature withdrawals.
  • Transparency in Donations : Limits anonymous donations to religious trusts that are not engaged in social services.

What It Means for You

  • For taxpayers — from individuals to corporations—this represent a watershed moment.
  • The bill promotes a simpler, more transparent tax system with fewer compliance hurdles
  • The changes usher in clarity, efficiency, and digital integration, making compliance smoother while aiming to reduce disputes and litigation. The law is expected to be operational from 1 April 2026, subject to Rajya Sabha approval and Presidential assent
